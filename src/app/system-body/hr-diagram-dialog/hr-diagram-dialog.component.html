The Hertzsprung–Russell diagram plots stars by surface temperature (hot on the left) against brightness (luminous at the top). Most stars lie along the main sequence — the diagonal band shown — where they spend the bulk of their lives fusing hydrogen.

A star's mass sets both where it sits on the main sequence and how long it stays there: massive O- and B-type stars blaze through their fuel in millions of years, while cool K and M dwarfs last far longer than the present age of the universe. An age well outside that expected window is flagged above.
